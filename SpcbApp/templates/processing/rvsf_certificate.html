Annexure-I
Terms and Conditions
  1. The RVSF shall receive unfit vehicles or End-of-Life vehicles for scrapping and must carry out treatment, depolluting, dismantling, segregation and scrapping activities in accordance with the provisions of the relevant laws.
  2. The RVSF shall send all the recovered and segregated materials from End-of-Life vehicles to the registered recyclers or refurbishers, co-processors for recycling and reuse of components or materials, in case of not having recycling or refurbishing facility.
  3. The RVSF shall send all non-recyclable or non-refurbishable materials and non-utilizable hazardous materials to Common Hazardous Waste Treatment, Storage and Disposal Facility authorised under Hazardous and Other Wastes (Management and Transboundary Movement) Rules, 2016.
  4. Extended Producer Responsibility certificate generated by the Registered Vehicle Scrapping Facility can be purchased by a producer through the centralised online portal to meet the Extended Producer Responsibility obligations under Environment Protection (End-of-Life Vehicles) Rules, 2025.
  5. The RVSF shall generate the EPR certificates after the verification of recycling facility by SPCB/PCC/CPCB.
  6. Extended Producer Responsibility certificate shall be generated subject to the accounting of recycling and refurbishing of all other End-of-Life vehicle materials and environmentally sound disposal of non-recoverable or non-recyclable or non-refurbishable hazardous materials.
  7. The RVSF shall exchange the Extended Producer Responsibility certificates upon processing of every End-of-Life vehicle based on the quantity of steel recovered from the End-of-Life vehicles on the centralized online portal developed by CPCB.
  8. The quantity eligible for generation of Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) Certificate shall be calculated as per the formula provided in the Environment Protection (End-of-Life Vehicles) Rules, 2025.
  9. An Extended Producer Responsibility certificate generated by the Registered Vehicle Scrapping Facilities shall be valid for a period of five years for meeting the obligations of producer under these rules.
  10. The RVSF shall submit the information regarding quantity of End-of-Life Vehicles procured & scrapped; scraps recovered, recycled, refurbished, disposed; EPR Certificates generated & sold and any other such relevant information on the portal.
  11. The RVSF shall file quarterly returns in the prescribed form on the centralized online portal by 30th day of the next month of the previous quarter and keep a record of the same.
  12. The RVSF shall ensure that the facility and recycling processes are in accordance with the standards or guidelines laid down by the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) and/ or Ministry of Road Transport & Highways, Government of India.
  13. The RVSF shall comply with the provisions of the Hazardous and Other Wastes (Management and Transboundary Movement) Rules, 2016; Environment Protection (End-of-Life Vehicles) Rules, 2025; and amendments made under thereof.
  14. The Board reserves right to cancel/amend/revoke the registration at any time as per the policy of the Board or Government.
  15. The RVSF fails to comply with the provisions relating to handling and scrapping of End-of-Life vehicles in environmentally sound manner under these rules, thereby causing loss, damage or injury to environment or public health, shall be liable to pay environmental compensation as per the guidelines issued by the CPCB.
  16. The provision of EC as stipulated under provision 15 of Chapter VII of Environment Protection (End-of-Life Vehicles) Rules, 2025 shall be applicable in case of any violation/noncompliance.
  17. In case of any violation of the above condition, your registration will be suspended/ cancelled.
